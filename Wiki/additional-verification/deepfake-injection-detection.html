Deepfake and Injection Attack Detection

What is Deepfake and Injection Attack Detection?

OkayLive+ is EMAS eKYC's advanced liveness detection component that specifically targets AI-generated fraud and sophisticated injection attacks. While standard liveness detection stops basic spoofing attempts like photos and videos, OkayLive+ goes further—detecting deepfake content, AI-generated images, and injection attacks that manipulate camera feeds at the system level. This cutting-edge capability protects businesses against the most sophisticated fraud threats that standard verification systems cannot detect.

Deepfake and injection attack detection represents the next generation of liveness verification, designed specifically to combat AI-powered fraud and technical exploitation. This advanced technology analyzes selfies and video streams for telltale signs of synthetic media creation, AI generation artifacts, and system-level manipulation that bypasses standard security measures.

Unlike basic liveness detection that identifies printed photos, replay videos, or physical masks, OkayLive+ uses specialized AI algorithms trained to recognize the subtle patterns that distinguish AI-generated content from authentic human captures. It also detects injection attacks where fraudsters manipulate camera drivers, virtual cameras, or system processes to inject pre-recorded or synthetic video directly into the verification stream—completely bypassing the physical camera.

What Problems Does It Solve?

Deepfake Technology Has Become Accessible to Fraudsters

What once required Hollywood-level resources can now be accomplished with consumer hardware and free software. Fraudsters use AI face-swap technology to create realistic videos where they appear as someone else, or generate completely synthetic faces that don't exist. These deepfakes can fool facial recognition systems and even basic liveness detection that checks for blinking or movement, because the AI-generated video includes these natural behaviors. Without specialized deepfake detection, businesses cannot distinguish between legitimate users and AI-generated imposters.

Injection Attacks Bypass Traditional Camera-Based Verification

Sophisticated fraudsters use injection attacks to feed pre-recorded or synthetic video directly into the verification system without using the physical camera at all. They install virtual camera software, modify camera drivers, or use API hooking to intercept the video stream. To the verification system, this injected video appears identical to legitimate camera input. Standard liveness detection cannot detect these attacks because it assumes the video feed originates from a physical camera capturing a real person in real-time.

AI-Generated Images Are Indistinguishable to Human Reviewers

Modern AI image generation creates synthetic faces that look completely realistic to the human eye. These AI-generated selfies include natural lighting, skin texture, and facial features that manual reviewers cannot identify as fake. Businesses relying on human review or basic image analysis are completely vulnerable to this type of fraud, which is particularly dangerous in high-value transactions where criminals invest resources into sophisticated identity theft.

Standard Liveness Detection Was Not Designed for AI Threats

Traditional liveness detection focuses on detecting printed photos, digital screens, masks, and video replays—physical presentation attacks. The technology was developed before AI-generated content and injection attacks became prevalent threats. These legacy systems lack the specialized analysis capabilities required to detect synthetic media or system-level manipulation, creating a critical security gap that organized fraud rings actively exploit.

The Arms Race Between Fraud and Detection Is Accelerating

As businesses deploy stronger verification measures, fraudsters adapt with more sophisticated techniques. What worked yesterday may not work tomorrow. Businesses need detection capabilities that evolve alongside fraud techniques, with AI models continuously trained on emerging threats. Without advanced threat intelligence and regular updates, verification systems become obsolete, exposing businesses to next-generation attacks they cannot detect.

OkayLive+ adds specialized detection for AI-generated content, deepfake videos, and system-level injection attacks. Most businesses use standard OkayLive effectively. OkayLive+ is designed for high-value transactions, high-security environments, and industries experiencing sophisticated organized fraud.
When should businesses deploy OkayLive+ versus standard liveness?
