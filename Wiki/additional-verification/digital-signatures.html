Digital Signatures

What are Digital Signatures?

Digital Signatures is a partner service available through EMAS eKYC that enables businesses to collect legally binding electronic signatures on documents and agreements remotely. This capability transforms traditional paper-based signing processes into instant digital workflows where customers can review, sign, and submit documents from their smartphones or computers without physical paperwork, in-person meetings, or postal mail.

Unlike simple image captures of handwritten signatures, true digital signatures use cryptographic technology to verify signer identity, ensure document integrity, and create tamper-evident records that meet legal requirements across jurisdictions. Each signature is linked to the signer's verified identity and timestamped, creating an audit trail that proves who signed what document and when—providing stronger legal standing than traditional wet signatures on paper.

As part of the EMAS eKYC ecosystem, Digital Signatures integrates seamlessly with identity verification workflows, allowing businesses to verify customer identity and capture legally binding agreement in a single continuous process. This combination supports remote account opening, loan closings, insurance policy sales, and any scenario requiring both identity verification and documented customer consent.

What Problems Does It Solve?

Physical Paperwork Creates Bottlenecks and Delays

Traditional document signing requires printing contracts, mailing them to customers, waiting for signed copies to return by post, and manually filing physical documents. This process can take days or weeks, during which deals may fall through, customers may change their minds, and time-sensitive opportunities are lost. For businesses processing thousands of agreements monthly, the administrative overhead of managing physical documents, storage, and retrieval becomes a significant operational burden that scales poorly with growth.

Geographic Limitations Prevent Deal Completion

When customers and businesses are in different locations, completing signature-required transactions becomes complicated and expensive. Sending documents via courier, scheduling in-person signing appointments, or requiring customers to visit physical branches creates friction that causes deal abandonment. Rural customers, international clients, and mobile populations face particular challenges—a loan applicant shouldn't need to travel hours to a bank branch just to sign documents, and insurance companies shouldn't lose sales because agents can't physically meet every prospect.

Signature Verification Lacks Accountability and Security

Paper signatures are easy to forge and difficult to verify. Businesses have limited ability to confirm that the person who signed a document is actually who they claim to be, opening opportunities for fraud and identity theft. When disputes arise, proving signature authenticity requires expensive handwriting analysis, and documents can be altered after signing without detection. This lack of accountability exposes businesses to fraud losses and legal challenges while making contract enforcement more difficult.

Document Management and Compliance Are Resource-Intensive

Physical documents require secure storage, organized filing systems, and retrieval processes that become unwieldy as volume grows. Businesses must maintain fireproof storage facilities, implement retention policies, and have staff locate specific documents when needed for audits or legal proceedings. Regulatory compliance often requires proving when documents were signed, by whom, and under what circumstances—evidence that's difficult to establish with paper signatures and manual record-keeping. The costs of storage space, document retrieval, and compliance management compound over years of accumulated paperwork.

FAQ

Are digital signatures legally binding?
Yes, digital signatures are legally binding in most countries worldwide. Laws like the U.S. ESIGN Act, EU eIDAS Regulation, and similar legislation in Asia, Latin America, and other regions recognize electronic signatures as equivalent to handwritten signatures. Cryptographic digital signatures often provide stronger legal standing than paper signatures because they create tamper-evident records proving who signed, when, and that the document hasn't been altered since signing.
How do digital signatures differ from electronic signatures?
"Electronic signature" is a broad term covering any electronic method of indicating agreement, including typing your name, clicking "I agree," or capturing a signature image. "Digital signature" specifically refers to cryptographic signatures that use mathematical algorithms to bind a signature to a document and verify signer identity. Digital signatures provide stronger security and legal standing because they detect any document tampering and cryptographically link to the signer's identity.
Can customers sign documents on mobile devices?
Yes, digital signature platforms are specifically optimized for mobile devices. Customers can review documents on smartphones or tablets and sign with their finger or stylus. The mobile experience is designed to be simple and intuitive, with documents formatted for small screens and signature capture optimized for touch interfaces. Most digital signatures today are captured on mobile devices rather than computers.
What prevents someone from denying they signed a document?
Digital signatures create comprehensive audit trails that make repudiation extremely difficult. The system records the signer's IP address, device information, timestamp, and authentication method used. When integrated with identity verification like EMAS eKYC, the signature is tied to verified identity with facial recognition and document checks. This evidence—combined with cryptographic proof that the signature originated from the signer's device—provides overwhelming proof of signature authenticity that courts recognize.
How long does the signing process take?
For simple agreements, customers can review and sign documents in under 2 minutes. More complex contracts like loan agreements or insurance policies that require thorough review may take 10-15 minutes. However, this is still dramatically faster than traditional processes involving printing, mailing, and document return, which take days or weeks. The digital process eliminates waiting time while allowing customers to review documents carefully.
What happens if there's a technical problem during signing?
Digital signature platforms are designed with robust error handling and recovery. If internet connectivity is lost, the system saves progress and allows customers to resume where they left off. If customers close the browser or app accidentally, they receive email or SMS reminders with secure links to return and complete signing. Technical support is available to assist with any issues, and businesses can configure fallback options like manual document collection for edge cases.
How are signed documents stored and accessed?
Signed documents are stored securely in encrypted digital archives with access controls restricting visibility to authorized parties. Businesses can retrieve signed documents instantly through administrative dashboards, eliminating the time and cost of locating physical files. Storage systems comply with data protection regulations and can be configured with automatic retention policies. For regulatory compliance, audit trails documenting all access to signed documents are maintained.
