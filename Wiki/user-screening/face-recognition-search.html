Face Recognition Search

What is Face Recognition Search?

OkayFace Search is EMAS eKYC's facial recognition search tool that enables businesses to search their entire customer database using facial biometrics. Unlike traditional database searches that require exact name matches or ID numbers, OkayFace Search identifies individuals by comparing a captured face image against all stored customer profiles, returning potential matches with confidence scores.

This capability transforms how businesses detect duplicate accounts, identify fraud networks, and verify customer identities across their systems. Whether investigating suspicious activity, preventing multiple account creation, or finding customers who have changed their names, OkayFace Search provides instant answers that would be impossible through conventional database queries.

Built on the same AI-powered facial recognition technology as OkayFace verification, the search function delivers 99.5% accuracy while processing queries against millions of records in seconds.

What Problems Does It Solve?

Duplicate Accounts Drain Resources and Enable Fraud. Fraudsters create multiple accounts using different names, email addresses, and phone numbers to exploit promotional offers, bypass bans, or build synthetic fraud networks. Traditional database searches cannot detect these duplicates because they rely on matching names or ID numbers that fraudsters easily change. Businesses waste money on duplicate promotional bonuses and struggle to enforce terms of service.

Identity Changes Make Customers Impossible to Find. Customers who legally change their names after marriage, divorce, or personal preference become invisible in traditional name-based database searches. When these customers contact support, apply for new services, or attempt to recover accounts, staff cannot locate their records. This creates poor customer experiences, duplicated accounts, and compliance issues.

Manual Fraud Investigation Is Slow and Incomplete. When fraud investigators suspect a customer is using multiple accounts or is connected to known fraudsters, they must manually review hundreds of customer photos looking for visual matches. This process takes hours or days, introduces human error from fatigue or bias, and often misses sophisticated fraud networks.

Account Takeover Victims Cannot Prove Their Identity. When customers report their accounts have been taken over and credentials changed, support teams struggle to verify the legitimate owner. Traditional recovery methods rely on remembering passwords, answering security questions, or accessing email accountsâ€”all controlled by the attacker. Without ability to biometrically search the database, businesses cannot definitively identify the real customer.

Fraud Networks Operate Undetected Across Multiple Identities. Organized fraud rings create networks of seemingly unrelated accounts using different personal information. Traditional fraud detection cannot connect these accounts because names, addresses, and contact information differ. Fraud losses accumulate across many accounts before patterns emerge, by which time criminals have already profited and disappeared.

How Businesses Use It

Financial Services and Digital Banking

Banks use OkayFace Search to prevent customers from opening multiple accounts to exploit promotional bonuses and credit offers. During new account applications, systems search the customer database for facial matches to existing accounts. When matches are found, banks can enforce rules limiting promotional offers to one per person or investigate potential fraud.

E-Commerce and Marketplace Platforms

Online marketplaces deploy OkayFace Search to prevent sellers banned for fraud or policy violations from creating new merchant accounts under different names. When new sellers register, platforms search for facial matches against banned user databases. Matches trigger enhanced verification or automatic rejection, preventing bad actors from circumventing enforcement actions.

Gaming and Entertainment Platforms

Gaming companies use facial search to enforce account bans and prevent cheaters from returning under new identities. When players create accounts, systems check against databases of banned users. Matches indicate ban evasion attempts, allowing platforms to maintain fair play environments. This also prevents users from creating multiple accounts to exploit referral bonuses.

Insurance Companies

Insurers search facial databases during claims processing to detect fraud rings where multiple people file false claims using different identities. By comparing claimant photos across all policies and claims, investigators identify individuals involved in multiple suspicious claims under various names. This uncovers organized fraud networks invisible to name-based searches.

Telecommunications

Mobile operators use facial search to prevent customers from obtaining multiple SIM cards under different names for use in SMS spam operations or fraud schemes. During SIM registration, systems search for facial matches to existing customers. Unusual patterns of one person registering many SIM cards trigger investigation.

FAQ

Q: How fast can OkayFace Search query large customer databases?
A: OkayFace Search processes queries against databases of millions of customer profiles in under 3 seconds. The system uses optimized facial recognition algorithms and indexing to deliver real-time search results even for very large databases. Performance scales efficiently as customer databases grow.
Q: What confidence thresholds should businesses use for matches?
A: Match confidence thresholds depend on use case and risk tolerance. For duplicate detection, 95%+ confidence typically indicates strong matches. For fraud investigation, 85-95% confidence warrants manual review. For account recovery, 98%+ confidence ensures accurate identification. Businesses can configure thresholds based on their specific needs and acceptable false positive rates.
Q: Can facial search work with low-quality photos?
A: OkayFace Search achieves best results with clear, front-facing photos similar to those captured during identity verification. It can match lower-quality images like profile pictures or security camera footage, but accuracy decreases with poor lighting, extreme angles, or low resolution. The system returns confidence scores indicating match reliability.
Q: How does this prevent false matches between similar-looking people?
A: OkayFace Search uses advanced AI algorithms that achieve 99.5% accuracy with a 0.000001 false match rate. The system analyzes multiple facial features and biometric markers beyond superficial appearance. Match confidence scores help businesses set appropriate thresholds, and suspected false positives can be reviewed manually.
Q: Is facial search compliant with privacy regulations?
A: Facial search must be implemented in compliance with GDPR, PDPA, and regional biometric privacy laws. Businesses must obtain customer consent for biometric data collection and use, implement secure storage with encryption, provide clear privacy policies, and allow customers to access and delete their biometric data. EMAS eKYC provides privacy-compliant infrastructure.
Q: Can this integrate with existing fraud detection systems?
A: Yes, OkayFace Search offers REST APIs for integration with fraud detection platforms, CRM systems, and customer support tools. Businesses can trigger searches programmatically during onboarding, as part of fraud investigation workflows, or through support interfaces. The system returns structured data including match confidence and customer records.
Q: How do you prevent misuse of facial search capabilities?
A: OkayFace Search includes access controls, audit logging, and usage monitoring to prevent misuse. Businesses can restrict search capabilities to specific user roles (fraud investigators, senior support staff), log all searches for audit purposes, and implement approval workflows for sensitive searches. These controls ensure facial search is used appropriately and compliantly.
